Chapter Five — The Method

How an analyst
thinks.

01
Define the question first
Before opening a dataset, write down exactly what you need to know and why it matters. A vague question produces a vague answer.
02
Understand the data's biography
Where did this data come from? Who collected it? What could be wrong with it? Every dataset has a backstory that shapes how you should trust it.
03
Clean with decisions, not defaults
Dropping a null and filling a null are different choices. Every cleaning decision should be documented and justified it's the analyst's first set of assumptions.
04
Explore until something surprises you
The finding you didn't expect is often the most valuable one. EDA shouldn't stop at distributions and correlations it should run until the data has nothing left to hide.
05
Visualise one idea per chart
Every chart must communicate a single insight. If you need a legend explaining three different colour dimensions, the chart has failed before the viewer starts reading.
06
Tell the story to someone non-technical
If you can't explain the insight to someone without a data background, you don't yet understand it well enough yourself. Clarity of communication is evidence of clarity of thinking.
